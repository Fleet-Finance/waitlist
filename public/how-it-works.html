How yield is split between FYC & FFC ▸ show
FYC has a capped APY (the effective cap). Every month, FYC receives just enough loan interest to reach its cap — nothing more. All remaining loan interest flows to FFC. This is why FFC typically grows faster: it earns the residual after FYC is satisfied. When no loans are active, both tranches grow equally from USYC yield alone.
Understanding φ (phi) coverage ratio ▸ show
φ = V_FFC ÷ outstanding loans. It must stay at or above 0.80 for new loans to be originated. FFC acts as the first-loss layer — for every $1 of loans deployed, at least $0.80 of FFC value must be backing it. If φ drops below 0.80 due to defaults or FFC redemptions, new loan originations are automatically blocked until V_FFC recovers.

⚠️ Default Waterfall
FFC (Junior tranche) absorbs all losses first — FYC price stays constant while FFC has buffer
Only if FFC is fully drained does FYC (Senior) take any write-down
Vehicle auction recovery (55–80%) is already netted out before the pool is affected
Insurance token burns can restore FYC price if a write-down occurs
Expand any active loan card in the centre panel and click Simulate Default to trigger one manually and watch the waterfall in real time
💡 Yield Mechanics
Both tranches always earn USYC T-bill yield on undeployed capital, even with no active loans
FYC earns loan interest up to its effective cap — all residual above the cap flows to FFC
The 12M APY shown is a projection based on the current month's actual yield — it updates every tick
This is a simulation only — no real tokens, wallets, or transactions are involved
